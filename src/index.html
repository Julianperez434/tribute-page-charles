A tribute to

Charles Babbage

1791-1871

Charles Babbage (1791-1871) was an English mathematician, philosopher, and inventor who is credited with designing the first mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine.

Babbage was born in London and studied at Cambridge University. He was interested in developing machines that could perform mathematical calculations automatically and accurately.

Although he was never able to complete the construction of the Analytical Engine during his lifetime, his work laid the foundation for the development of modern computers.
